About Our Chapter

Thank you for visiting the webpage for the Epsilon Nu Chapter of Pi Kappa Phi at California State University, Sacramento. You'll find variety of information about our chapter and a lot of photos showing off our chapter. For any questions you may have, please refer to the contact tab located above.

"THE SUCCESS OF PI KAPPA PHI CAN BE ATTRIBUTED TO TWO THINGS. FROM THE BEGINNING WE HAD THE RIGHT PRINCIPLES. BUT IN THAT REGARD, WE WERE NO DIFFERENT FROM OTHER ORGANIZATIONS WHICH START BRAVELY BUT FALL. YES, IN 1904 WE SPARKED THE IDEA, BUT IT IS TO THOSE MEN WHO CAME AFTER US THAT WE OWE OUR WONDERFUL PI KAPPA PHI... WE HAVE GIVEN YOU THE PAST AS WE KNOW IT. THE FUTURE WILL LEAVE WITH YOU."

On a quiet December evening in 1904 in Charleston, S.C., three friends--Andrew Kroeg, Simon Fogarty and Harry Mixson--made a choice to lead. Those three men chose to start their own fraternity. The three grew to seven; seven grew to eight; eight grew to 11. From the College of Charleston, they spread to nearby Presbyterian in 1907--and then to California in 1909. In just over four years, Pi Kappa Phi had become a truly national fraternity. Over the course of a century, a fraternity with humble beginnings has grown into a brotherhood of more than 100,000 strong--all because three men made a choice to lead.

Our chapter was founded on February 27, 1982. The chapter thrived at Sac State amassing over 200 alumni before being closed on October of 1996. Epsilon Nu lay dormant until Spring 2012, when the national fraternity decided to bring Pi Kappa Phi back to campus. After over a year of preparation and hard work, the chapter was re-founded on November 16, 2013. Since then the chapter has added to the history of Epsilon Nu and continues to grow and build it's presence at Sac State and in the Sacramento community.
